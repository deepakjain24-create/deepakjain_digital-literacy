**Project Report Section: Poor Digital Communication Scenario**

Poor digital communication can often lead to misunderstandings, conflicts, and even serious professional or academic consequences. One situation that illustrates this is a hypothetical scenario involving a college group project. In this situation, a group of students was working together on an important presentation. One of the group members sent a message in the group chat saying, “This part is wrong, someone fix it,” without specifying what exactly was wrong or who should fix it. The message sounded rude and unclear, which led to confusion and frustration among the group members. Some students felt criticized, while others ignored the message because they thought someone else would handle it. As a result, the mistake was not corrected on time, and the group submitted an incomplete presentation, which affected everyone’s grades.

The main problem in this situation was poor digital communication. The message was not clear, polite, or specific. Digital communication lacks tone and facial expressions, so messages can easily be misunderstood. A short or direct message may sound rude even if the sender did not intend it that way. This situation could have been avoided if the student had communicated more clearly and politely. For example, the student could have written, “I think there might be a mistake in slide 4 regarding the data. Can someone please check and correct it? Let me know if you need help.” This message is polite, specific, and cooperative, which would have encouraged teamwork and avoided confusion.

This example shows that responsible digital communication is very important in academic and professional environments. People should always be clear, respectful, and specific when communicating online. They should also review their messages before sending them to ensure the tone is appropriate. Good digital communication helps maintain teamwork, prevents misunderstandings, and ensures that work is completed efficiently.
