
# Digital Literacy Project – README

## Project Title: Digital Literacy and Responsible Digital Communication

## Project Overview

This project was completed as part of a Digital Literacy course to understand and practice responsible digital communication, email etiquette, social media responsibility, online safety, cybercrime awareness, and digital payment fraud awareness. The project focused on real-world digital communication scenarios and safe online behavior for college students.

The purpose of this project was not only to learn theory but also to apply digital communication skills in practical situations such as writing professional emails, understanding cyber fraud, and learning how to stay safe online.

---

## Tasks Completed in This Project

### Task 1: Professional Email Writing (Email Etiquette)

In this task, two professional emails were written:

1. Email to a professor requesting an assignment deadline extension.
2. Email to an internship coordinator expressing interest in a summer internship.

These emails followed professional email rules:

* Clear subject line
* Professional greeting
* Well-structured body
* Polite tone
* Proper closing and signature

This task helped in understanding formal communication and professional email writing.

---

### Task 2: Responsible Social Media Use Checklist

A checklist was created containing Do’s and Don’ts for responsible social media usage for college students.

#### Key Do’s:

* Think before posting
* Verify information before sharing
* Use privacy settings
* Maintain a positive digital footprint
* Respect others online

#### Key Don’ts:

* Do not share personal information
* Do not spread fake news
* Do not engage in cyberbullying
* Do not post offensive content
* Do not overshare personal life online

This task helped in understanding digital responsibility and online behavior.

---

### Task 3: Case Study on UPI / Online Payment Fraud

A case study was written explaining:

* What UPI fraud is
* How the fraud happens step-by-step
* Who is usually targeted
* Consequences of online payment fraud

The study showed that most cyber fraud happens due to social engineering where fraudsters trick users into sharing OTPs or approving UPI payment requests.

---

### Task 4: Stay Safe Online Checklist

A safety checklist was created for college students to prevent cybercrime and online fraud.

#### Important Safety Tips:

* Use strong passwords
* Enable two-factor authentication
* Never share OTP or UPI PIN
* Do not click unknown links
* Avoid public Wi-Fi for banking
* Download apps from official stores only
* Log out from shared computers
* Keep devices updated

#### Cyber Crime Reporting in India:

* National Cyber Crime Portal: **cybercrime.gov.in**
* Cyber Crime Helpline: **1930**

This task helped in understanding cyber safety and preventive measures.

---

### Task 5: Reflection on Online Safety and Cybercrime

A report was written describing:

* What was surprising while researching cybercrime
* Personal habits to change for better digital safety
* Importance of digital awareness and responsible online behavior

The reflection highlighted that most cybercrime happens due to lack of awareness rather than technical hacking.
