## Project Report Section: Online Safety and Cybercrime Awareness (400–500 Words)

While researching the topic of online safety, cybercrime, and digital fraud, I learned many things that were both surprising and concerning. Before working on this topic, I believed that cybercrime mostly involved highly skilled hackers breaking into systems using advanced technology. However, during my research, I discovered that most cybercrimes, especially in India, do not involve complex hacking at all. Instead, they rely on **social engineering**, where criminals trick people into giving away their personal information, OTPs, passwords, or approving UPI payments themselves. This was the most surprising thing I learned — that many cyber frauds happen because of human mistakes rather than technical weaknesses.

I also learned that college students are one of the common target groups for cyber fraud because they frequently use online payments, social media, online shopping, and public Wi-Fi networks. Many students use the same password for multiple accounts, click unknown links, or ignore privacy and security settings. Fraudsters often send fake messages related to internships, part-time jobs, scholarships, KYC updates, bank account suspension, or prize winnings to trick students into clicking malicious links. Once the victim enters their login details or OTP, the fraudsters gain access to their accounts and transfer money.

Another surprising thing I learned was about the **Cyber Crime Helpline 1930** and the **National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in)**. I did not know before that if online payment fraud is reported immediately, sometimes the bank or authorities can freeze the transaction and help recover the money. This shows how important it is to report cybercrime quickly instead of ignoring it due to fear or embarrassment.

After researching this topic, one habit I will personally change is that I will be much more careful before clicking any links or responding to urgent messages related to banking, UPI, or account verification. I will also enable two-factor authentication on all important accounts such as email, banking apps, and social media accounts. Another habit I will adopt is regularly updating passwords and not using the same password for multiple accounts. I will also avoid using public Wi-Fi for logging into banking or payment apps.

This research made me realize that online safety is not only a technical issue but also a matter of awareness, behavior, and responsibility. Most cybercrimes can be prevented if people follow basic digital safety practices. Therefore, digital literacy and cyber awareness are very important for college students and young internet users in today’s digital world.
