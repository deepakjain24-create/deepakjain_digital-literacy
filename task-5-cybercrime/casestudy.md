## Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud (Real Incident with Source)

A real case of UPI fraud was reported in Delhi where fraudsters carried out a “UPI hijack scam” and stole around ₹20 lakh from a victim’s bank account. The fraud happened after the victim lost his mobile phone, and the criminals used the phone to access banking and UPI apps and perform unauthorized transactions. 
(The Times of India Report)

### What the Crime Is

UPI fraud is a cybercrime where criminals steal money using digital payment platforms by tricking users, stealing phones, accessing OTPs, or sending fake payment requests. The fraud usually does not involve hacking banks but instead exploits user mistakes and security weaknesses.

### How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

In the Delhi case, the fraud happened in the following way:

1. The victim lost his mobile phone.
2. The fraudsters obtained the phone and reset it.
3. They accessed banking apps linked to the phone number.
4. They reset the UPI PIN using Aadhaar-linked authentication.
5. They transferred money from the victim’s bank account to fake accounts.
6. The victim later received transaction alerts and reported the fraud.
   Police later traced the fraudsters and arrested them. (The Times of India Report)

### Who Is Usually Targeted

UPI fraudsters usually target:

* Students
* Elderly people
* Online shoppers
* Small business owners
* People who lose their phones
* People unfamiliar with digital payment security

These groups are targeted because they may not be fully aware of cyber fraud techniques.

### Consequences

The consequences of UPI fraud are serious:

* Victims lose large amounts of money
* Recovery of money is difficult
* Mental stress and anxiety
* Loss of trust in digital payments
* Legal and police involvement

Reports show that UPI fraud cases have increased significantly in India, with thousands of incidents reported every year and large financial losses. 
(Business Standard Report)

### Conclusion

This case study shows that UPI fraud is a growing digital crime and users must protect their phones, never share OTP or UPI PIN, and immediately report fraud to the bank and cybercrime portal.
